- The Reserve Bank of India has introduced new directions on pre-payment charges on loans, primarily aimed at protecting Micro and Small Enterprises (MSEs) and individual borrowers from excessive charges.
- Starting January 1, 2026, regulated entities (REs) including commercial banks, cooperative banks, NBFCs, and All India Financial Institutions will face restrictions on charging pre-payment fees for floating rate loans, with specific exemptions outlined for smaller loans and specific financial institutions.
